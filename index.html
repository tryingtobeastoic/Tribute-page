Dr. Jordan B Peterson

One of the most influential psychologists of the 21st century

Life

  • 1962 - Born in Edmonton, Alberta
  • 1975 - Joined the NDP (New Democratic party), a socialist party, at age 13, and was actively involved in canadian politics until he was 18
  • 1976 - Ran for Vice President of the NDP in Alberta at age 14, and came within 14 votes of being elected
  • 1979 - Entered Grande Prairie Regional College to study political science and English literature, studying to be a corporate lawyer. During this time he read The Road to Wigan Pier by George Orwell, which significantly affected his educational focus and worldview. He later transferred to the University of Alberta
  • 1982 - Completed his BA in political science from the University of Alberta
  • 1983 - Took a year off to visit Europe, where he began studying the psychological origins of the Cold War; 20th-century European totalitarianism; and the works of Carl Jung, Friedrich Nietzsche, Aleksandr Solzhenitsyn, and Fyodor Dostoevsky
  • 1984 - Returned to the University of Alberta and received a BA in psychology
  • 1985 - Moved to Montreal to attend McGill University
  • 1991 - Earned his PhD in clinical psychology under the supervision of Robert O. Pihl, and remained as a post-doctoral fellow at McGill's Douglas Hospital until his departure, working with Pihl and Maurice Dongier. While at McGill University and the Douglas Hospital, Peterson conducted research into familial alcoholism and its associated psychopathologies, such as childhood and adolescent aggression and hyperactive behaviour
  • 1993 - Joined Harvard University as an assistant professor, teaching and conducting research, in the psychology department, later becoming an associate professor. Author Gregg Hurwitz, a former student of Peterson's at Harvard, has cited Peterson as an inspiration of his, and psychologist Shelley Carson, former PhD student and now-professor at Harvard, recalled that Peterson's lectures had "something akin to a cult following", stating, "I remember students crying on the last day of class because they wouldn't get to hear him anymore
  • 1998 - Returned to Canada and eventually became a full professor at the University of Toronto
  • 1999 - Published Maps of Meaning: The Architecture of Belief in which Peterson describes a comprehensive theory about how people construct meaning, form beliefs, and make narratives. The book, which took Peterson 15 years to complete, draws concepts from various fields including mythology, religion, literature, philosophy, and psychology, in accordance to the modern scientific understanding of how the brain functions.
  • 2003 - Began to create his public presence by appearing on Big Ideas on TVOntario
  • 2004 - Gets a 13-part TV miniseries based on Maps of Meaning: The Architecture of Belief aired on TVOntario
  • 2005 - Established a website and company, with colleagues Daniel M. Higgins and Robert O. Pihl, to deliver an evolving writing therapy system called The Self-Authoring Suite. Peterson states that more than 10,000 students have used the program, with drop-out rates decreasing by 25 per cent and grade point averages rising by 20 per cent. The Future Authoring program has been used with McGill University undergraduates on academic probation to improve grades, and since 2011 by the Rotterdam School of Management, Erasmus University.
  • 2013 - Registered a YouTube channel named JordanPetersonVideos and immediately began uploading recordings of lectures and interviews
  • 2016 - Released the first installment of a three-part lecture video series, entitled "Professor against political correctness: Part I: Fear and the Law, sparking his rise to international prominence. In the video, he stated that he would not use the preferred gender pronouns of students and faculty, saying it fell under compelled speech, and announced his objection to the Canadian government's Bill C-16, which proposed to add "gender identity or expression" as a prohibited ground of discrimination under the Canadian Human Rights Act, and to similarly expand the definitions of promoting genocide and publicly inciting hatred in the hate speech laws in Canada. He also appeared in The Joe Rogan Experience (#877) for the first time
  • 2018 - Published his internationally bestselling book 12 Rules for Life: An Antidote to Chaos, translated into some 50 languages, and went on a world tour, spanning more than 160 cities, to promote the book
  • 2020 - Returned home in August after overcoming a horrific benzodiazepine withdrawal syndrome and eventually resumed podcasting
  • 2021 - Resigned from the University of Toronto, becoming professor emeritus, and published his 3rd book Beyond Order: 12 More Rules for Life, a sequel to his previous book. It was also translated to some 50 languages and sold over 750,000 copies
  • 2022 - Currently touring internationally to promote his newly published book

Academic career

Peterson's areas of study and research within the fields of psychology are psychopharmacology, abnormal, neuro, clinical, personality, social, industrial and organizational, religious, ideological, political, and creativity. Peterson has authored or co-authored more than a hundred academic papers and was cited almost 8,000 times as of mid-2017 and more than 18,000 times as of 2022. This makes him one of the top 50 most cited clinical psychologists of all time. His h-index is 57.

It is estimated that after 20 years a "successful scientist" would have an h-index of 20, an "outstanding scientist" would have an h-index of 40, and a "truly unique" individual would have an h-index of 60.
